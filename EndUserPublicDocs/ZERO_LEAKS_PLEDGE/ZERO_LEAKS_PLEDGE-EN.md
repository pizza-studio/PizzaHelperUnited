# Pledge on Zero Use & Zero Leaks of NDA-Protected Game Test Client Content

> All translations of this document are for reference purposes only. The original Simplified Chinese version of this document is the only legally binding version. This document constitutes the self-regulatory declaration and project code of conduct for Pizza Studio (hereinafter referred to as "the Developer" or "our team"). Although not formally a legal contract in judicial proceedings, it serves as the highest guiding principle for the compliance development and community management of our projects.

This statement is the compliance policy promulgated by Pizza Studio, applicable to "The Latte Helper" (hereinafter referred to as "this application") and retrospectively applicable to the discontinued precursor, "The Pizza Helper" version 5.x.

**Last Updated: March 8th, 2026**

---

## Article 1: Purpose and Stance

### 1.1 Background and Necessity

Given the significant negative impact on the overall game community and developers caused by recent systematic thefts and malicious leaks of unreleased game content (such as the HomDGCat lawsuit, Case No. 1:26-cv-00676-SEG), the Developer hereby solemnly declares:

This application is strictly unaffiliated with any "insider/leaker" perpetrators who steal, disseminate, or exploit unreleased game data. The Developer **strictly dissociates and draws a clear line** against such actions that violate Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and infringe upon trade secrets.

### 1.2 Effectiveness and Modification

This statement takes effect on the date of publication. The Developer reserves the right to revise this statement at any time based on legislative changes, industry practices, or practical needs, and such updates shall be published in the project repository. Users and contributors bear the obligation to periodically review the latest version. Continued use of this application or participation in this project implies agreement with and acceptance of the then-current terms of this statement.

---

## Article 2: Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" as referred to in this statement is strictly limited to the following two categories of **NDA-protected game client data**:
- **Game client** data content directly constrained by miHoYo's / Cognosphere's Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) terms.
- **Game client** data content not voluntarily and publicly provided by miHoYo.

> The Developer **respects the legal legitimacy of the NDA as a confidentiality mechanism**. Our objection is directed solely at "**the act of leaking NDA-protected game client content**", not the NDA system itself.

"Non-Confidential Information" refers to the officially released game version data. Among such data, content that was originally confidential but matches the official release shall lose its confidential status and become non-confidential information starting from the date miHoYo pushes the "official game client pre-download data." However, any game storyline content included in the aforementioned pre-download data legally remains Confidential Information.

> Game storyline content is not necessary for the functional operation of this application, and therefore will not be, and does not need to be, called upon.

## Article 3: Substantial Damages Caused by Leaks

The Developer is well aware that the premature disclosure of NDA-protected game test client content or unreleased resources is by no means "simple information sharing." Instead, it constitutes a **systematic attack and substantial damage** against the original development team (miHoYo / Cognosphere Pte. Ltd.) across multiple dimensions. Such damages include, but are not limited to:

### 3.1 Direct Economic Losses

- **"Premature Fatigue" Effect**: When players learn about character skills or storylines in advance, they may feel "fatigued" or find the content "below expectations," leading them to abandon their consumption plans and directly causing a decline in revenue from new character banners.
- **Impact on Marketing Conversion**: Leaks render official pre-release promotions ineffective; the sunk costs invested fail to generate expected conversion benefits, resulting in a futile waste of marketing resources.

### 3.2 Disruption of Business Models and Promotional Pacing

- Premature exposure severely interferes with the official marketing plans, causing carefully orchestrated "elements of surprise" to drastically depreciate.
- It destroys the core operational model of "generating attention through game version updates," forcing publishers to alter version scheduling abruptly and thereby increasing operational costs.

### 3.3 Commercial Competition and Security Risks

- Systematically datamining and publishing NDA-protected unreleased **game client** data equates to exposing core business secrets (including character designs, numerical systems, storyline progressions, and version roadmaps) to potential competitors.
- Competitors may utilize this intelligence for early planning, strategy adjustments, or even targeted poaching, thereby weakening the original developer's market-exclusive advantages.

### 3.4 Poisoning of the Community Ecosystem

- **Collapse of Trust**: Leaked content is often taken out of context, leading to biased evaluations and misunderstandings among players, sparking community disputes, and severely degrading a healthy ecological environment.
- **Loss of Surprise**: Players’ prior knowledge of storylines, characters, and events drastically diminishes the sense of novelty and the sheer joy of exploration upon official release.
- **Compromised Fairness**: Prematurely published game test client data grants some players asymmetric information advantages, disrupting a fair competitive environment within the game.

### 3.5 Suppression of Creative Enthusiasm

Unauthorized leaking that blatantly disregards intellectual property rights will severely damage the creative willingness of upstream game designers and development teams, potentially reducing the supply of high-quality game titles and negatively impacting the broader game industry.

## Article 4: Self-Regulatory Conduct and Commitments

To prevent the aforementioned damages to the original developer and to maintain a sound player ecosystem, this application commits to adhering to the **highest standards of self-regulatory compliance**:

### 4.1 Refusal to Feature NDA-Protected Game Test Client Content

This application **does not possess, securely store, or display** any **game client content** that is NDA-protected and has not yet been publicly released on the official game client (including but not limited to: character stats, storyline texts, art assets, and future version roadmaps).

### 4.2 Strict Prohibition on Illegal Datamining of NDA-Protected Game Clients

The Developer **strictly does not participate** in any cracking, decompiling, unpacking, or circumvention of encryption mechanisms targeting **NDA-protected game test clients** (including game clients not officially released as stable-public-releases); nor do we engage in technical exchanges or data sharing with any organization or individual involved in such acts.

### 4.3 Strict Prohibition on Inducement of Violations

The Developer **strictly refrains from instigating, inciting, or inducing** any internal testers who have signed NDAs to leak confidential information.

## Article 5: Compliance and Transparency of Data Sources

All game version-related data and assets utilized by this application are ensured to be sourced **publicly, securely, and completely free from NDA risks**:

### 5.1 Officially Released Information

- Information proactively published by the official developers via social media (e.g., HoYoLAB), preview livestreams, or official announcements.
- Content already showcased to the public by miHoYo (e.g., "Drip Marketing" character reveals, preview programs, and promotional trailers).

### 5.2 Content Available in Official Releases

- Real-time client content of officially launched versions accessible to any player.
- As a general principle, third-party datamining results derived from the **official game client pre-download data** (not game test client data) released by the game development team one to two days prior to an update launch may be used, subject to the following restrictions:
  1. Any game storyline content within such data falls outside the scope of processing by this application to prevent the risk of spoilers. The functional implementation of this application similarly does not require invoking such information.
  2. In the case of *Honkai: Star Rail*, usage is strictly limited to the content available on the day the new version goes live.
- If a public provider of datamined resources is suspected of illicitly mining NDA-protected data, the Developer hereby declares: **no interaction with, nor utilization of, such illicit data** shall occur.

### 5.3 Technical Considerations for Rejecting Confidential Data

In addition to the aforementioned legal risks, another key reason for rejecting non-official game client data is **technical reliability**. Datamined figures from non-official environments (such as game test clients) are highly uncertain and subject to frequent changes before the official launch. Such volatile data cannot serve as a reliable basis for offering precise character stat calculations or stable auxiliary features within this application.

### 5.4 Integration of Third-Party APIs

- **Public Character Showcase Interface APIs** (such as Enka.Network): These APIs strictly do not access NDA data; they only relay character showcase data that players have voluntarily made public.

## Article 6: Project Protection Mechanisms and Contributor Rules

As an open-source and community-driven project, the Developer has instituted **stringent rules and technical protection mechanisms** for the project's development workflow and community contributors:

### 6.1 Technical Protections and Review Mechanisms

- **Submission Restrictions and Asset Update Pipelines**: The GitHub repository for this application solely accepts Pull Requests pertaining to source code modifications; it utterly refuses any submissions implicating game version data. Updates to game assets are uniformly executed by the Developer’s internal, dedicated Continuous Integration (CI) pipeline. This pipeline enforces data-filtering mechanisms to thwart unauthorized confidential data from infiltrating the outputs.
- **Content Review and Leak Prevention**: The Developer conducts periodic content reviews to ensure the holistic compliance of data sources. Should any potential discrepancy be discovered, please promptly contact `contact@pizzastudio.org`. The team commits to addressing such issues within 72 hours.

### 6.2 Ban on Submitting Leaked Content

It is strictly prohibited for anyone to directly submit, indirectly disseminate, cite links to, or implicitly suggest the following contents using Issues, Pull Requests, project discussion boards, or within any related communities:

- **Game Test Client Data**: Any data related to Beta tests or closed network tests.
- **Unreleased Content**: Unreleased character intelligence, unimplemented story texts.
- **Confidential Information**: Any confidential information defined within Article 2 of this statement.

Contributors or users violating this ban shall solely bear all resultant legal and breach-of-contract liabilities.

### 6.3 Contributor Authorization and Warranties

Given that the project repository does not accept Pull Requests pertaining to game version data—restricting submissions strictly to code modifications—all participants (including those submitting Issues or PRs, joining discussions, or contributing code) must make the following declarations and warranties:

- **Content Compliance**: All submitted content (including source code, discussions, and proposals) does not encompass any confidential information defined in Article 2 of this statement.
- **Development Originality**: The submitted code was not developed using any NDA-protected techniques, figures, or data as a basis.
- **Prohibition of Inducement and Distribution**: The participant has never disseminated, cited, or implied confidential information in any form (directly or indirectly) within the project community, nor incited someone else to violate NDA terms.
- **Liability for Damages**: Should the submitted content violate the aforementioned warranties, entangling the project or the Developer in legal disputes or causing reputational/property damage, the defaulting contributor shall bear absolute legal responsibility and fully compensate the Developer for all direct and indirect damages sustained.

### 6.4 Handling of Feature Requests

If a user requests the application to prematurely adapt to "unreleased content only known via leaks," the request will be **summarily closed and definitively rejected**, without exception.

### 6.5 Sanctions for Violations

For individuals who repeatedly submit NDA-related content in violation of the rules, the Developer may enforce a **permanent ban**, revoking their access to participate in the project. The Developer further reserves the right to gather evidence for presentation to judicial authorities in the event of ensuing litigation.
